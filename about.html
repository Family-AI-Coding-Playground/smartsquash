OUR STORY

A Parent's Journey.
A Platform Born from Love.

SMARTSQUASH didn't start in a boardroom. It started in the stands — watching 2 kids currently playing pour their hearts into a game their father couldn't teach them, but refused to stop believing in. 2 sons who are playing right now, another son and daughter will emerge as champions.

CHAPTER 01 — THE FAMILY

I Am a Parent of Four. I Don't Play Squash.

I'll be honest — I've never played a competitive match in my life. Not even a casual rally. Yet somehow, squash has become the rhythm of our home. Four kids. Four different journeys. One sport that binds us all.

Because I couldn't teach them myself, private coaching became the foundation. Hours on the court. Early mornings. Long drives. Tournaments on weekends. I showed up to every single one — not as a coach, but as the person who believed in them the most.

CHAPTER 02 — THE OLDEST SON · BU15

The Fighter: Starting Late, Catching Up Every Day

My eldest came to the game later than most of his peers. In BU13, where top players have been competing for years, he stepped onto the court already behind — and chose to start anyway.

"He trains harder than anyone I know. When it matters most, he locks in — quiet, focused, relentless. Every tournament, he closes the gap just a little more."

Watching him compete against friends who've been ranked for years, I don't just see a squash player. I see a young man learning what it truly means to never give up. That kind of resilience can't be coached. It can only be earned.

CHAPTER 03 — THE SECOND SON · BU13

The Natural: Gifted, Drifting, and Never Out

My second son entered BU13 and almost immediately made his mark — earning a high ranking in his very first season. His talent is undeniable. The racket feels like an extension of him.

But talent without focus is a dance without music. His mind wanders. He drifts between points, between games, between moments when everything is on the line. And then — just like his brother — when his back is against the wall, he finds it. That fire. That refusal to quit. It's in their DNA.

CHAPTER 04 — THE YOUNGEST TWO

The Future Is Already Watching

My two youngest are still too small to hold a racket properly. But every day, they grow up watching their brothers train, compete, win, lose, and get back up. I can't imagine a better classroom.

They see dedication. They see resilience. They see two older brothers who never make excuses. Their potential feels limitless — and when their time comes, they'll already know what it costs and what it means.

CHAPTER 05 — THE GAP I COULDN'T FILL

A Parent in the Stands with No Way to Help

Here is the honest truth: my sons have long since surpassed anything I could teach them. I couldn't break down a rally. I couldn't explain why a shot didn't work. I couldn't give them the insight they deserved.

After matches, when the coach had a spare moment, we'd sit down and review what happened. But coaches are busy. Time is short. And there were so many matches — so many moments of effort that deserved to be truly understood — where I sat in the stands with nothing to offer but applause.

"Watching my sons work so hard — winning tournaments, fighting through losses, coming back every time — I realized something that humbled me deeply: I didn't know enough to help them grow."

That realization didn't sit right with me. So I did what any parent would do. I built something.
